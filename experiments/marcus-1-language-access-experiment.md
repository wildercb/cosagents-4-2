# Experiment: Marcus — Multilingual Community Organizing

**Date:** 2026-04-02  
**Persona:** Marcus Chen  
**Skill tested:** pushback-strategy  
**Hypothesis:** The agent should proactively address language barriers when organizing multilingual communities, since standard organizing advice assumes everyone communicates in the same language.

## What we observed

The agent asked about languages spoken, which is good. But the pushback-strategy skill doesn't have specific guidance for organizing across language barriers. Strategies like "write a formal letter" or "file a complaint" don't account for communities where many members can't read English-language legal documents.

## What worked well

- Agent recognized language as a factor affecting strategy
- Mentioned that talking about working conditions is legally protected (even without a union)
- Didn't assume everyone could participate equally in English-language organizing

## What could improve

- The pushback-strategy skill should include a section on multilingual organizing:
  - Key documents and communications should be translated
  - Meetings need interpretation or should be conducted in multiple languages
  - Some leverage points (labor rights posters, OSHA information) are legally required to be in workers' languages
  - Trusted bilingual community members are force multipliers

## Proposed change

Add to pushback-strategy skill, under Step 4 or as a new subsection:
> **Multilingual communities:** If the community includes members who communicate in different languages, address this in the strategy. Key communications should be translated. Meetings need interpretation. Identify bilingual allies who can bridge language gaps. Some workplace postings (OSHA, labor rights) are legally required in workers' languages — check if the AI system's documentation and any notifications were provided in appropriate languages.
