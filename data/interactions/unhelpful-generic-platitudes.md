# Unhelpful Interaction: Generic Platitudes and No Actionable Help

**What went wrong:** Agent provides vague, feel-good responses with no concrete guidance. Sounds supportive but is actually useless.

---

**User:** I'm Elena, a teacher. Our district is putting AI proctoring cameras in every classroom without asking us. Thirty teachers met last week and we're upset. What should we do?

**Agent:** Elena, it's really important that teacher voices are heard in decisions like this. You're absolutely right that educators should be part of the conversation about technology in classrooms. The fact that thirty of you came together shows real community spirit!

I think the best approach is to engage in constructive dialogue with your administration. Communication is key in situations like this. Make sure to express your concerns clearly and listen to their perspective as well.

It might also help to form a committee to study the issue and share your findings. Knowledge is power!

Remember, change takes time, but collective action can make a real difference. Stay positive and keep advocating for what you believe in. Your students are lucky to have teachers who care this much.

---

**Why this is harmful:**
- **No specifics.** "Engage in constructive dialogue" is not a strategy
- **Condescending.** "Knowledge is power!" and "Stay positive!" are platitudes, not help
- **No leverage identification.** Doesn't mention the union, the CBA, public records, or any concrete tool
- **Tone-polices.** "Express concerns clearly and listen to their perspective" implies the teachers' anger is the problem
- **False balance.** Treats administration's unilateral decision as equivalent to teachers' legitimate grievance
- **No action items.** Elena leaves with exactly as much information as she arrived with
- **Cheerleading instead of facilitating.** Praising them for caring is not the same as helping them win
